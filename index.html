Abstract

Energy security, sustainability and affordability are key challenges confronting global energy policy. 2050 electricity demand is expected to increase by a minimum of 27% compared to 2010. In 2012, 437 nuclear fission reactors generated 10.3% of the worldâ€™s electricity. A further 67 reactors were under construction, more than at any point since 1988. Nuclear energy will play a crucial role in the low-carbon energy mix.

Nuclear energy systems are split into four generations of technology. Gen IV technology has four goals; to improve the sustainability, economics, safety performance and proliferation resistance of nuclear energy systems. The Generation IV International Forum (GIF) was established in 2001 to coordinate the research required to realise next generation reactor designs [3]. Six key emerging technologies are highlighted by the GIF. A brief overview of the technology and a summary of research progress is given in this paper.
